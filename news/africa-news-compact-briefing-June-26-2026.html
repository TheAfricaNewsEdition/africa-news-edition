DRC Outbreak

DRC Faces Record-Breaking Ebola Outbreak Amid Humanitarian Crisis

Ebola outbreak: Healthcare workers and medical response teams mobilized across the Democratic Republic of Congo as case numbers surge to unprecedented levels.
The DRC's current Ebola outbreak has recorded the highest number of confirmed cases in the first month of any African outbreak, with officials confirming over 1,000 cases and 267 deaths as of June 23. The rare Bundibugyo virus strain has spread to at least three displacement camps in eastern Congo, with 25 confirmed cases. The Africa CDC reports that response funding needs have surged to $1.4 billion—nearly triple earlier estimates—as humanitarian crisis conditions worsen containment efforts. Meanwhile, the U.S. is providing experimental antibody drug MBP134 for compassionate use and clinical trials, with tests of Gilead's remdesivir beginning soon. The first Ebola case outside Africa was confirmed in a French doctor who returned from the DRC, prompting new 21-day travel restrictions for those leaving affected areas.

Sources: WHO, Africa CDC, DW, TRT Turkey, RFI, The Guardian, Eastleigh Voice

Kenya Domestic Politics

Massive Police Presence as Kenya Marks Two Years of Gen Z Uprising

Kenyan police deployed heavy security forces on June 25 to prevent protests marking two years since the landmark 2024 Gen Z uprising that challenged economic hardship and corruption. The demonstrations had mobilized youth across ethnic lines, forcing parliament to cancel new taxes, but resulted in at least 127 deaths over two years according to a police watchdog. Despite President William Ruto's acknowledgement of citizens' right to demonstrate, authorities deployed thousands of hired armed men to prevent looting and property destruction during past protests.

Source: AFP

Kenya Health Policy

Kenya Halts U.S.-Backed Ebola Quarantine Center for Americans

Kenya's health minister suspended construction of a U.S.-planned Ebola quarantine facility designed to isolate exposed Americans. The Trump administration had proposed sending Americans exposed to Ebola abroad to the Kenya facility instead of returning them home. A high court had halted the project in May following local protests that resulted in three deaths. The minister apologized for continuing construction despite the court order and was found in contempt.

Source: AP

South Sudan Elections

South Sudan Sets First Election Date Since 2011 Independence

South Sudan has announced December 22 as the date for its first general election since independence, following years of repeated postponements due to ongoing armed conflict. The election remains overshadowed by political tensions between President Salva Kiir and opposition leader Riek Machar, who faces unresolved treason charges. The opposition has expressed concerns that unresolved disputes with the government make elections dangerous to pursue.

Source: AP

Sudan Refugees

Libya Bans Sudanese Entry, Orders Deportations Amid Rising Hostility

Libya's eastern-based government has issued a decree banning citizens from Sudan, Eritrea, Ethiopia, and Somalia from entering, targeting thousands of Sudanese refugees. Refugees report severe hardships including verbal abuse, physical assaults, armed threats, and growing hate speech. Movement restrictions force many to remain indoors, with landlords helping hide valuables and identification to protect residents from authorities.

Source: Sudan Tribune

Sudan Refugees

Sudan's War Refugees Face Harsh Crackdown in Egypt Jails, Rising Deportations

Egypt, which took in over a million Sudanese after war erupted in 2023, has escalated a crackdown on refugees facing economic crisis and anti-migrant sentiment. Plain-clothes security officers have detained thousands in unmarked vehicles, with authorities deporting over 5,500 people since November—a sharp rise from roughly 100 formal deportations annually in 2023–2024. Lawyers and human rights groups report systematic arrests and mass deportations despite Egypt's stated role as a safe haven.

Source: Reuters

Sudan War Crimes

UN Report Documents Widespread Sexual Violence in Sudan Conflict

A new UN human rights report verifies 546 incidents of conflict-related sexual violence across 16 of Sudan's 18 states since war erupted in April 2023. Sexual violence has been consistently used to terrorize civilians, spreading alongside conflict and displacement routes. Most verified incidents were attributed to men in Rapid Support Forces uniforms, affiliated fighters, and Arab militias, leaving profound long-term impacts on victims, families, and communities.

Source: UN News

Sudan Humanitarian Emergency

Over 215 Civilians Dead in South Darfur Detention Center

215+
Deaths in May-June
More than 215 civilians detained at Daqrees prison in Nyala, South Darfur, have died over two months due to torture and disease outbreaks, according to the Sudan Doctors Network. The facility houses large numbers of civilians detained since war broke out in April 2023. Deaths resulted from lack of healthcare, mistreatment, torture, and epidemic spread. Humanitarian organizations face severe restrictions accessing detainees.

Source: Sudan Tribune

Sudan Humanitarian Emergency

El-Obeid City Faces Desperate Conditions as RSF Offensive Looms

Over half a million displaced people in El-Obeid face severe shortages of food, drinking water, and medicine as paramilitaries close in and fears mount of a major RSF offensive. The city, a strategic link between central Sudan and Darfur, has been overwhelmed by arrivals since the war began. EU and U.S. officials warned the RSF against imminent attack, with multiple countries expressing grave concern and drawing parallels to past atrocities.

Source: The New Arab, Sudan Tribune

DRC Armed Conflict

Amnesty Flags DRC Army Links to Armed Group Accused of Torturing Civilians

Amnesty International has linked the Congolese army (FARDC) to a local armed group accused of torturing civilians, looting property, and abducting women as sexual slaves. The CMC FDP, part of the Wazalendo coalition used by the army as proxy forces against the M23 movement, maintains bases and targets civilians at night. After clashes with M23, fighters have taken revenge on family members with perceived M23 links—acts that violate international humanitarian law and may constitute war crimes.

Libyan Warlord Convicted of Crimes at Detention Center

Ossama Anjiem, a Libyan warlord who led Tripoli's detention facility, was sentenced to seven years and four months for violating inmates' rights through torture, cruelty, and degrading treatment. The ICC had issued an arrest warrant in January 2025 for crimes against humanity and war crimes including murder, torture, rape, and sexual violence since 2015. EU Migration Policy

Amnesty Criticizes EU for Expanding Migration Cooperation with 'Xenophobic' Libya

Amnesty International condemns the EU's plans to expand migration cooperation with Libyan authorities and armed groups amid escalating campaigns of racially discriminatory arrests and unlawful collective expulsions. The rights group says the EU is already "complicit in horrific violations" through financial support for the Libyan coast guard, which routinely intercepts migrant vessels. Extending cooperation to eastern armed groups with war crimes records shows "shocking disregard for international law and human life."

Source: Le Monde, Amnesty International

Mozambique Gas Project

Investigation Reveals Systematic Sexual Assault and Detention at TotalEnergies LNG Site

More than two dozen survivor accounts have reconstructed events in Palma, Mozambique, where hundreds fled after al-Shabab-linked insurgents took control near TotalEnergies' LNG facility in June 2021. Military commandos systematically sexually assaulted women at Patacua military base, then detained approximately 200 men in shipping containers at Quatro Caminho—an area under the Joint Task Force protection. South Africa's Supreme Court of Appeal has ruled in favor of former Zambian President Edgar Lungu's family in a burial dispute, more than a year after his June 2025 death. Zimbabwe Constitutional Crisis

Zimbabwe Moves Toward 'Constitutional Coup' With Extended Presidential Terms

Zimbabwe's parliament has voted to amend the constitution, extending presidential terms from five to seven years and allowing President Emmerson Mnangagwa to stay in office until 2030. The controversial change will also replace direct presidential elections with parliamentary appointment. Opponents call it a "constitutional coup," though the government says it will bring stability. The president is expected to sign it into law next month.

Source: The Guardian

Niger Counterterrorism

Niamey Airport Attack Tests Niger Junta as Terrorists Adapt Tactics

Terrorists claimed the lives of 11 soldiers and two civilians in a new assault on Niamey International Airport, using deception to pose as passengers and employing taxis and minibuses for access. Security had been strengthened after a January attack, but the new approach suggests earlier infiltration to observe and exploit weaknesses. Recent military demolition of neighborhoods around the airport may have also shaped attackers' strategies.

Nigeria Civil Society

Women Leaders in Maiduguri Work to Steer Youth Away from Gang Violence

Women and community leaders in northeastern Nigeria are tackling gang violence through dialogue instead of security crackdowns alone. For years, youth gangs called "Marlians" have terrorized Maiduguri and Jere with knife and machete attacks. Local women, former gang members, and civil society groups have held regular dialogue sessions since 2018, attempting to persuade young men to walk away from violence in parallel with government enforcement efforts.

Africa Economics

Iran War Threatens Billions in Critical Remittances to Africa

$124B
2024 Gulf Remittances to Africa
Remittances from the roughly 30 million foreign nationals working in the Gulf—most from Africa and South Asia—face unprecedented pressure from the Iran war. The estimated $124 billion in 2024 remittances have slumped significantly since conflict began: Kenya's central bank reported an 18% drop in April transfers, while average values on Africa's largest digital payment platforms fell sharply. Egypt, Ethiopia, Rwanda, Somalia, and Sudan are among the largest recipients at risk.

Source: Semafor

Africa International Relations

African States Prioritize Economic Interests, Back Beijing Over Taiwan

Kenya's deportation of a Taiwanese environmental scientist highlights a broader African trend: governments increasingly see adherence to the One China policy as aligned with economic and diplomatic interests. Kenya deported the official over passport recognition, mirroring most African governments' positions. South Africa downgraded Taiwan's liaison office from Pretoria to Johannesburg, while Mauritius, Seychelles, and Madagascar reportedly denied air navigation permits to Taiwan's leader during planned diplomatic travel.

2026 FIFA World Cup

Refugee-Born Players Carry War Stories to World Cup 2026

Global stage: Players born in refugee camps across Africa now represent nations on football's biggest stage, their journeys reflecting resilience amid displacement and conflict.
Among the 2026 World Cup's diverse squad, several players carry historic stories of war and displacement. Alphonso Davies (Canada) was born in Ghana's Buduburam refugee camp after his parents fled Liberia's civil war, resettling in Edmonton at age five. Mohamed Toure (Australia) was born in a Guinea refugee camp after his family fled Liberia, waiting 14 years for resettlement in Adelaide. Awer Mabil (Australia) was born in Kenya's Kakuma refugee camp after his South Sudanese parents fled civil war, resettled in Adelaide at ten—he scored the penalty sending Australia to the 2022 World Cup. Nestory Irankunda (Australia) was born in Tanzania's Kigoma camp after his parents fled Burundi's civil war; scoring against Türkiye was "a dream come true." Antonio Rudiger (Germany) was born in Berlin to a mother who fled Sierra Leone's civil war in 1991. Eduardo Camavinga (France) was born in an Angola refugee camp after his parents fled DR Congo war.

Source: AlJazeera
