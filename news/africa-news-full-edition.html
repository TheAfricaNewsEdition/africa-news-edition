Democratic Republic of Congo / Public Health
DR Congo Reports 956 Confirmed Ebola Cases, 247 Deaths
Confirmed Ebola cases in the DRC have risen to 956, including 247 deaths, according to the latest situation report from health authorities. Twenty-three new confirmed cases, two fatal, were reported in Ituri and North Kivu.
Ituri remains the epicentre with 874 cases and 201 deaths — over 91% of the national total. Ninety-two patients have recovered; 361 remain in isolation or hospital, and 162 suspected cases were identified in a single day. Contact tracing sits at 69.3%, below the 95% target.
Health Minister Roger Kamba said the outbreak remains in an upward phase, with more cases expected as teams expand active case-finding deeper into affected communities.

DRC / Bunia
At Least 30 Deaths at a Congo Camp Show Ebola Could Be Spreading Fast
At least 30 people have died since early May in one camp for displaced civilians in north-eastern Congo — a death rate camp officials called unprecedented, with some deaths confirmed as Ebola. Causes could not be verified for all because families in Kigonze camp in Bunia had until recently refused testing.
Aid workers said the spike shows how communities are now more exposed as donors — including the United States under President Donald Trump — cut funding for the water, hygiene and sanitation essential to fighting a disease spread through bodily fluids.

17 Medics Dead and 70+ Infected Since the Outbreak Began
Seventeen medics have died and the toll has passed 200 in an outbreak tearing through a health system already weakened by conflict, displacement and chronic underfunding. WHO said 75 health workers had contracted the virus since the outbreak was declared on 15 May.
Basic protective equipment remains scarce, with some facilities struggling to secure gloves and masks needed to limit infection.
Suiting up. Health workers prepare protective equipment at an Ebola treatment centre in eastern DRC, where shortages of gloves and masks have left staff dangerously exposed.

Egypt • South Sudan / Preparedness
Neighbours Tighten Precautions as the Outbreak Spreads
Egypt has strengthened public-health precautions after the expansion of cases in Central Africa, leading to the postponement or cancellation of several international gatherings. Officials insist there is no domestic outbreak but call precaution necessary given rising regional travel.
In South Sudan, health authorities in Morobo County reinforced measures after a suspected case died within 24 hours in the DRC’s Ingbokolo area, just 20km away. The country has recorded no linked cases but is classed as high risk due to porous borders.
The African Export-Import Bank cancelled its annual shareholder meeting in Egypt over outbreak concerns, conducting business remotely — one of the most significant disruptions to major African business events since the outbreak was declared.

DRC / Goma
The Parable of the Tshukudu, Goma’s Quintessential Transport
The true symbol of Goma is not a man with a rifle but the tshukudu — an idiosyncratic, man-powered wooden scooter. Narrow enough to zigzag through markets and able to bear more than 500kg, at $200 with no petrol costs it beats a motorcycle.
The war between Congo and M23 is hurting business, and cheap motorised tricycles from India and China compete for heavy loads. Yet the emblem endures — in souvenirs, and in a statue at the centre of town.

Could Eritrea Come In From the Cold?
Shunned by the West since 1991, Isaias Afwerki may be edging toward rehabilitation. Trump’s top Africa adviser reportedly met him in late 2025, and Secretary of State Marco Rubio has spoken of a ‘new era’ in relations.
Washington may want mining access, help against the Houthis, or to avert an Eritrea–Ethiopia war that could rock the Red Sea and merge with Sudan’s civil war. Isaias, for his part, worries about Ethiopia’s Abiy Ahmed.
