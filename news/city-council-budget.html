City council passes contested transit budget

The city council on Tuesday passed a transit budget that more than doubles bus frequency on six high-demand routes, capping three nights of public hearings that drew the largest civic turnout in over a decade.

The 7–4 vote followed a final amendment that protected late-night service on the Riverside line, a route that organizers had argued was the difference between a working schedule and a stranded one for hundreds of hospital staff.

What changed

The approved budget redirects roughly $14 million from a planned downtown parking expansion into operating funds for the transit authority. Officials said new buses are already on order, with the first arriving in the fall.

For the people who depend on this system, a fifteen-minute bus is a different kind of city than a forty-minute bus.

Opponents on the council, led by the south-district representative, argued the funding model leans too heavily on a single year of surplus revenue and warned of cuts in 2027 if collections soften.

What comes next

Service changes take effect in two phases — the first in September, the second by the end of the year. The transit authority will hold open-house sessions in each affected neighborhood beginning next month.
