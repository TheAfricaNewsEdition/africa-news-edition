The harbor festival returns, quieter than before

The harbor festival opened on Friday to thinner crowds and a quieter waterfront than at any point in the last fifteen years. Organizers said attendance was down by roughly a third, and that the change felt less like a setback than a recalibration.

Forty-two vendors set up along the pier this year, compared with sixty-eight in 2024. The reduction was a deliberate choice: the city's new permit cap aimed to ease pressure on the boardwalk after two summers of safety complaints.

What people said

It feels like the festival we had when I was a kid. You can actually hear the water.

That sentiment, voiced by a longtime resident at the south entrance, was echoed by several visitors. Others were less pleased — fewer food stalls meant longer lines, and three of the music acts pulled out at the last minute citing the smaller stage.

The economics

Smaller crowds did not, by the festival's own count, mean smaller revenue. Per-vendor sales were up. The festival committee said it will release final figures next week.

The closing fireworks are scheduled for Sunday evening, weather permitting.
