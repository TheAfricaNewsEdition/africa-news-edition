Six books to carry you through a slow summer

A list, because the season asks for one. Half fiction, half not. Nothing so heavy you will resent it on a train, nothing so slight you will forget it by Sunday.

The list

1. A novel about an island that may or may not be remembered correctly. Short. Strange. The kind of book that gets passed between two friends until the cover begins to fray.

2. An essay collection on cities. Walks more than arguments. Best read with the windows open.

3. A biography of a minor 19th-century cartographer. Trust us. It is funnier than it sounds.

4. A second novel, this one loud. Three generations, one kitchen, an enormous fight at a wedding. The summer-by-the-pool book.

5. A slim book of poems. The kind you keep on the table and open at random.

6. One reread. Pick something you loved at twenty-two. See what survives.

The right summer book is the one you finish on a porch, not the one you finish on a flight.

Send us yours. We are building a longer list for August.
