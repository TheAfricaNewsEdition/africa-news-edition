Editor's Note
June 14, 2026

A new chapter for independent news

This paper started as a folder of notes and a stubborn idea: that a small newsroom, careful and unhurried, still has something to offer. We are not chasing the noise. We are reading the city, the books on our desks, and the conversations that find us, and we are writing what we see.

Every issue will carry a handful of stories. Some weeks will lean political, others cultural; the categories are guides, not fences. What every piece will share is a point of view, an editor who fought for it, and a writer who took the time to think.

What you can expect

Roughly one new story a week, sometimes two. Always at the top of this page. The featured piece is the one we most want you to read; the rest are arranged by date. Every story lives at its own page, which means you can share a single link without losing the rest.

We would rather publish four good stories a month than forty thin ones.

A note on the design

You will notice the paper is cream-colored, the rules are thin, and the type is set in Georgia. The intent is not nostalgia. The intent is to slow you down by half a beat, the way a good page should.

If you write to us, we will write back. Until then, thank you for being here.
