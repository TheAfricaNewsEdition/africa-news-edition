Ghana's World Cup "Unlucky Streak" Lives On

At the 2010 World Cup in South Africa, Ghana stood on the brink of history. With the quarter-final against Uruguay locked at 1–1 and only seconds remaining in extra time, Uruguay's Luis Suárez deliberately handled a goal-bound shot on the line, preventing what would almost certainly have been the winning goal and Africa's first-ever World Cup semi-final appearance.

World Cup Analysis

Ghana were awarded a penalty. Asamoah Gyan struck the crossbar. The match went to penalties, where Uruguay prevailed 4–2, ending one of the most heartbreaking chapters in African football history.

Fast forward to FIFA 2026 in Boston, and Ghana once again found themselves on the wrong side of controversy. Final result: England 0-0 Ghana.

England 0–0 Ghana, Boston 2026: Ghana's players react during controversial moments in a match defined by disputed officiating decisions that left supporters questioning fairness and consistency.

The first incident came when England's goalkeeper raced out of his area and appeared to deny a clear goalscoring opportunity. Many felt it warranted a red card, which would have reduced England to ten men. No card was shown.

The second was even more glaring. An England defender lunged into a Ghanaian attacker inside the penalty area. Replays appeared to show clear contact, yet no penalty was awarded, no VAR intervention materialised, and England remained at full strength.

To compound matters, FIFA's Man of the Match award went to England's Jude Bellingham. Even he seemed surprised by the decision. By any fair assessment, there were several Ghanaian players—goalkeeper Benjamin Asare chief among them—whose performances were more deserving of the honour.

And then there is the question of consistency. FIFA and domestic leagues have cracked down on players covering their mouths while speaking to teammates or officials, citing transparency concerns. Yet television cameras appeared to catch Bellingham doing exactly that on multiple occasions without consequence.

Football remains a beautiful game. But beauty alone cannot compensate for officiating decisions that leave players, supporters, and neutral observers alike wondering what might have been.

For Ghana, it was another painful addition to a catalogue of World Cup moments where fate, controversy, and heartbreak have seemed to arrive together.
